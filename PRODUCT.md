# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The product serves individuals who want a private, local-first way to understand, organize, and improve their financial life without connecting bank accounts or creating an account. Primary users include privacy-conscious people who prefer manual control, salary workers tracking monthly cash flow, freelancers managing variable income, and anyone who wants financial awareness without the tone or complexity of banking, accounting, crypto, or investment software.

Users are usually in a focused personal planning context: reviewing their month, adding transactions quickly, checking whether spending is on track, understanding cash flow, preparing a backup, or looking for actionable patterns in their financial behavior. They should feel organized, in control, confident, relaxed, and focused.

## Product Purpose

This is a local-first personal finance operating system: a personal finance workspace and money management dashboard for financial awareness. It stores data only on the user's device, avoids login and cloud dependency, and gives users clear ways to record transactions, manage accounts and budgets, analyze trends, generate reports, and back up their data.

Success means users can quickly track daily income and expenses, understand where their money goes, see savings and cash flow clearly, avoid going over budget, and trust that their financial data remains private.

**Financial management is the feature. Modern SaaS product experience is the identity.**

## Product Experience Goals

The application should feel like a modern SaaS product before it feels like a finance application.

Users should perceive:
- Premium
- Modern
- Fast
- Organized
- Professional

The application should feel closer to **Attio, Linear, Arc, Stripe, and Raycast** than to Mint, YNAB, QuickBooks, Xero, or any banking application.

## Screenshot Test

If a screenshot is shown to a startup founder for 3 seconds:

- They should assume it is a venture-backed SaaS product.
- They should NOT immediately recognize it as accounting software, banking software, an expense tracker, or a budgeting application.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel precise, calm, private, trustworthy, modern, and refined. Secondary traits are elegant, focused, premium, intelligent, professional, and analytical.

The experience should feel like a premium venture-backed SaaS platform that happens to manage money. Screens should be intentional, spacious, polished, and high-end; interactions should feel fast, lightweight, and effortless. The design benchmark is Attio, with supporting references from Linear, Arc Browser, Stripe Dashboard, Raycast, Dub.co, Vercel, Notion, and Framer.

The product should not make users feel overwhelmed, judged, punished, confused, or stressed. It should avoid scolding, gamified pressure, and noisy finance-report energy.

## Anti-references

Avoid banking application aesthetics: corporate navy and gold themes, traditional financial branding, financial-institution layouts, and institution-like trust signals.

Avoid crypto platform aesthetics: trading terminal layouts, candlestick-heavy dashboards, neon color schemes, and exchange-style interfaces.

Avoid accounting software aesthetics: ERP visual language, spreadsheet-first experiences, dense data tables everywhere, and feature-heavy admin surfaces.

Avoid gamified budgeting apps: mascots, achievement systems, reward animations, cartoon language, and moralizing progress metaphors.

Avoid outdated dashboards: KPI overload, too many cards, too many colors, excessive gradients, visual clutter, and report-like first impressions.

Specific product anti-references: Mint, YNAB, traditional banking apps, QuickBooks, Xero, crypto exchanges, and anything that looks like Excel with charts.

## Design Principles

1. **SaaS first, finance second.** A screenshot should read as a refined productivity product before it reads as a personal finance app.
2. **Whitespace over density.** Breathable layouts communicate quality. Prefer fewer, larger sections over grids of small cards.
3. **Typography leads hierarchy.** Size and weight carry the visual hierarchy. Color is used sparingly and purposefully.
4. **One accent color.** Green is the sole brand accent — used for primary actions, selected states, and positive financial signals only.
5. **Curate the financial picture.** Prioritize cash flow, spending trends, and recent activity over KPI blocks and data tables above the fold.
6. **Privacy visible but quiet.** Local-first and no-login should build trust without turning the interface into a privacy manifesto.
7. **Analysis should be calming.** Insights help users act with confidence. Never judge, punish, or alarm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. The application must support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, screen reader-friendly structure, reduced-motion preferences, and responsive layouts across mobile, tablet, and desktop.

The interface should be mobile-first where appropriate, with strong readability, clear hierarchy, and data visualizations that do not depend on color alone. Motion should be fast, subtle, purposeful, and state-driven, with reduced-motion alternatives.
